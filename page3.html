Nature an Importance of the Study


Cassava, Manihot esculenta Crantz, is a perennial woody shrub with an edible root, which grows in tropical and subtropical areas of thr word. It is also called yuca, manioc, mandioca and also known kamoteng kahoy or bilanghoy in local name. Cassava is the basis of many products, including food. It is mostly used for human consumption, and also used commercially for the production of animal feed and starch-based products (IITA, 2009).

The cassava industry in thr Philippines is now gaining momentum with the existence of various market opportunities. Cassava is grown not only for human food, but also for starch animal feed and industrial uses. For past twenty years cassava production in the Phillipines showed an irregular trend. In the late 1970s cassava productioon was at its peak. There was an increase of 108% area from 87,420 hectare in 1973 to 181,770 hectare in 1978, which result in a 356% increase intotal volume of production and a 120% increase in average yield (FOASTAT, 2001). The increase in the national average cassava yield 6.38 tons/ha. (Evangelio, 1998)

The Bureau of Agricultural Statistics (BAS) selected root crops such as cassava, sweet potato, gabi and ubi registered a total production of 16,073 metric metric tons. Cassava represents 45.36% of the total production with an average yield of 1.27 metric tons/hectare (http:samar.lgu-ph.com/econo.htm) (*2000).

In local scene, Samar in particular is an agricultural area, but not almost of the population is involve in cassava Farming. The researchers hence conduct this study to provide a sufficient knowledge on the growth and yield performance of different cultivars grown under local condition. However, this study was conducted to evaluate the performance of different cassava cultivars at local condition, Samar. The researcher wanted to improve the yield performance of cassava farming especially in local condition.

OBJECTIVE OF THE STUDY


1. To determine the growth and yield performance of different cassava cultivars.

2. To determine which cassavavariety produce high yield.

Time and Place of the Study

The study was conducted from April until December 2009 in the upland area located at Brgy. Geraga-an, Calbayog City, Samar.

Scope and limitation of the Study

This reseach focused on the growth and yield performance of different cassava cultivars such as T1 (Makan-1), T2(rayong-5), T3 (Lakan-1), and t4(Lakan-2) grown under Brgy. Geraga-an Calbayog City, Samar.
